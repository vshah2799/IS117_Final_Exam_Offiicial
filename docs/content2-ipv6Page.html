IPv6

IPv6 is the new and upcoming standard of IP addresses. Compared to IPv4 it is leaps ahead. An example of an IPv6 address is: 2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334. Unlike IPv4, IPv6 utilizes many more characters and can use both numbers and letters. An IPv6 address is 128bits and there are 340,282,366,920,938,463,463,374,607,431,768,211,456 possible combinations of IPv6 address. That is much more than we could ever use.

Why Have We Not Transitioned Yet?

The biggest problem of switching fully to IPv6 in all devices are routers. Most routers do not support the IPv6 protocol and it cannot be implemented through software. It is a hardware requirement for routers and this poses a financial problem. Most routers in the world are only IPv4 routers and to get millions of not billions of people to get new routers is very costly. Most people do not have the time to set up new routers or care about the effects that the IPV4 address shortage causes.
