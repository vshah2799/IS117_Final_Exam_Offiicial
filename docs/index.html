IPv4, IPv6 and Why It Should Matter To You

IP Addresses

IP addresses are one of the fundemntal parts of the internet. Any and every device that has access to the internet has one or even more. They enable users (servers, other computers etc.) and yourself to access other users through the internet.

How They're Used

A device, such as a computer, has an IP address assigned to it. This enables your router to connect to your computer. Your router also had an IP address which allows it to connect into the internet. Others and yourself are able to connect to the internet through this path and most IP addresses are under the IPv4 protocol.

The Problem Today

The problem today is that the IPv4 protocol has a small amount of unique numbers possible and no two devices can have the same IP address. The solution to that is the IPv6 protocol. It allows for a far greater number of unique IP addresses.
