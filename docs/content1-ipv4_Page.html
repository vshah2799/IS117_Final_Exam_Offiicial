IP and IPv4

IP Addresses
IP addresses connect users to the internet. Your router has an IP address in the IPv4 protocol. It is static which means it will not change. Your computer or other device also has one but it can be changed depending on the circumstances. Some routers are almost all new devices as in computers, phones, tablets, etc. have IPv6 addresses, too. IP addresses are literally the address of your routers and devices and this is why it crucial to gain an understanding of them.

IPv4

IPv4 addresses are 32 bit. They follow the simple form of XXX.XX.XXX.X. As simple and convient as this might be, IPv4 poses a major problem. There are approximately 4.3 billion possible IPv4 combinations and all devices that connect to the internet have them. Most only have IPv4 addresses and do not support IPv6. The problem with this is that 4.3 billion combinations is not enough for the needs of the world today. Millions of devices are made each year and sooner or later we will run out of IPv4 addresses.
